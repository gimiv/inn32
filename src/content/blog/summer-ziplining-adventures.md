## What is Canopy Ziplining?

Canopy ziplining is an adventure activity where you travel from point to point on cables strung between elevated platforms built into trees or on tall structures. Unlike the single-line ziplining found at some parks, authentic canopy tours feature multiple cables of varying lengths and angles, creating a complete course through the forest.

Alpine Adventures' course near North Woodstock combines ziplines with suspended bridges, sky-walks, and other aerial elements. The experience isn't just about speed—though you'll definitely experience plenty of that—it's about navigating terrain while taking in spectacular mountain views.

## The Alpine Adventures Experience

### Course Overview

Alpine Adventures' canopy course consists of multiple levels and difficulty ratings. Courses are designed so participants of varying experience levels can enjoy the experience. Beginner-friendly lines offer stability and scenic views. Intermediate and advanced lines provide speed and excitement for those seeking maximum adrenaline.

Most tours take 2-3 hours, including instruction time. You'll start with a full safety briefing, harness fitting, and practice runs on ground-level equipment before moving to actual ziplines. Professional guides accompany your group throughout, ensuring safety while providing commentary about the White Mountains ecosystem.

### What to Expect

**Safety Briefing** - Alpine Adventures takes safety seriously, and your tour begins with comprehensive instruction. You'll learn how to properly wear your harness, how to control your descent speed using friction gloves, and what to expect from each element of the course.

**The Ziplines Themselves** - Lines range from 200 to over 1,000 feet long. The longest and fastest lines provide heart-pounding drops through the canopy. The wind rushing past your ears, the view expanding as you accelerate, and the momentary weightlessness create an unforgettable sensation.

**Suspended Bridges and Sky-Walks** - Between ziplines, you'll traverse swaying suspension bridges and walk across elevated platforms called sky-walks. These aren't difficult technically, but they require focus and confidence. Your guide provides encouragement and tips for steady footing.

**Forest Immersion** - Throughout your tour, you're completely immersed in the White Mountains forest. You'll notice details most hikers never see: forest canopy structure, wildlife habitat, and the incredible complexity of old-growth forest ecosystems.

## Is Ziplining Right for You?

### Physical Requirements

Ziplining requires reasonable physical fitness, but you don't need to be an athlete. Most people who can climb stairs and maintain balance can complete a canopy course. Guides will ask about health conditions during your safety briefing—they're looking for information about conditions that might make ziplining uncomfortable, not reasons to turn you away.

Weight requirements exist for safety reasons. Alpine Adventures accommodates participants from around 50 to 300 pounds, but confirm specific limits when booking.

### Age Considerations

Children as young as seven or eight can usually participate in beginner-friendly courses, though specific age requirements vary. Teenage participants enjoy more challenging lines. Many families book group tours, making ziplining a memorable bonding experience.

### Fear of Heights

Some people with significant fear of heights complete zipline courses successfully because guides prepare you mentally and you're always securely connected to the cable. The first line might be intimidating, but by the third or fourth, most people find their confidence growing. Talk honestly with your guide about any anxiety—they understand and can help you work through it.

## Preparation and Packing

### What to Wear

- Athletic shoes with good grip and ankle support
- Quick-dry athletic clothing (you might sweat more than expected)
- Light layers for temperature changes in the forest
- Avoid loose jewelry that might snag
- Some people wear gloves to protect hands on friction gloves (optional but popular)

### What to Bring

- Water bottle (many courses allow water stops)
- Small backpack or hydration pack
- Camera for photos at the bottom (though you'll want to focus on the experience)
- Sunscreen and insect repellent

### Pre-Tour Preparation

- Eat a light meal 1-2 hours before your tour
- Stay hydrated throughout the day
- Get a good night's sleep—ziplining is more mentally demanding than it might appear
- Stretch briefly before arriving to warm up muscles
- Arrive 15-20 minutes early for check-in

## When to Book Your Zipline Tour

Summer is prime season for ziplining, with reliable weather and excellent visibility. July and early August offer the best weather conditions. June can be slightly buggy, while September provides cooler temperatures and fewer crowds.

Tours operate in spring and fall as well, but summer weather makes the experience most enjoyable. However, never assume summer weather is guaranteed—bring appropriate clothing regardless of the season.
